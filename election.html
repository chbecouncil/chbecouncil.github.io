2. Am I eligible to be nominated? Anyone who is enrolled as an undergraduate in CHBE is eligible

3. Can I run with a co-representative? Yes, a maximum of one other co-representative is allowed. You need only to submit ONE nomination for the both of you, but assign one as the point of contact for this election period.

4. I will be away on co-op, can I still run? Council rules indicate that one person must be available to attend meetings on campus during your term. If you are not in Vancouver and cannot attend meetings, your role must be forfeited.

However, if you are running with a co-representative who will be in Vancouver to fulfill these duties, then YES you can still run. As long as you coordinate this in advance (i.e., one co-rep will be in Vancouver for September 2017, and the other will be around for January 2018).

5. How long are election speeches? Maximum of 3 minutes for speeches, and 2 minutes for Q&A. This is still subject to change.

6. What is the time commitment per role? Time commitment will vary depending on how much you want to contribute and what your role is! If you want to find out more, contact anyone on the council about their specific role. You can find their contact information on the “Team” page.

Positions to be filled

Below are the descriptions of the roles and responsibilities of the members of the CHBE council.

President

  • Act as a chairperson for weekly club meetings.
  • Act as the primary representative to the EUS, AMS, APSC, CHBE departments, UBC, CsChE, and AiChE
  • Deal with endowment fund applications
  • Responsible for supporting council members in accomplishing their assigned duties
  • Liason to the student administrative commision

Vice President

  • Leads and supervises officers
  • Manages communication with all members
  • Supports all members where needed
  • Assumes duties of president when president is not present
  • Responsible for club property and spaces

Treasurer

  • Ensures club funds are distributed to reimburse members for club related expenses
  • Responsible for keeping up to date with funds, account balance and budget
  • Liaison to finance commision and EUS VP finance

Secretary

  • Take minutes for council meetings
  • Submit meetings minutes to EUS
  • Organize elections proceedings

Social Coordinator

  • Organize CHBE social events such as MexiCHEM, pub socials, samosa sales, pumpkin carving and others
  • Obtain liquor licenses for club
  • Collaborate with CHBE E-Week Officer for E-Week planning
  • Liaise directly with second year social officer
  • Main point of contact for E-week planning
  • Liaise directly with second year social officer
  • Attend EUS Events committee meetings (monthly/bimonthly)

Sports and Charity Coordinator

  • Organize recreational CHBE sports teams, including teams for UBC Rec events such as Storm the Wall and Day of the Longboat
  • Organize CHBE teams for interdepartmental competitions
  • Organize any other sports events such as watching sports matches
  • Participate in EUS Charity and Sports rep meetings
  • Organize CHBE Charity events

Industry Coordinator

  • Organizes Industry Night event and other career networking/recruitment related events
  • Be part of EUS co-op committee to provide feedback to students
  • Promote professional development activities organized by CHBE, EUS, APSC and UBC
  • Liaise directly with Industry Officer

Academic Coordinator

  • Organize and facilitate Beef and Pizza academic feedback sessions
  • Attend CHBE department curriculum meetings as the undergraduate student representative
  • Participate in the EUS curriculum committee and in EUS Annual General Meetings
  • Communicate curriculum and academic related issues to the council

Communications coordinator

  • Create weekly newsletters to be posted to the CHBE Club website and distributed to students
  • Manage and support social media
  • Create posters and social media banners for CHBE Club events
  • Keep clubroom calendar updated

Second year representative

  • Represent and communicate the views of second year students in meetings with both the council and with professors (as appropriate)
  • Communicates relevant council updates and events to second years
  • Organize any second year events as needed

Third year representative

  • Represent and communicate the views of third year students in meetings with both the council and with professors (as appropriate)
  • Communicates relevant council updates and events to third years
  • Collaborate with the department to organize third year field trip
  • Work with department to organize third year field trip
  • Organize any third year related events as needed

Fourth year representative

  • Represent and communicate the views of fourth year students in meetings with both the council and with professors (as appropriate)
  • Communicates relevant council updates and events to fourth years
  • Organize graduation photo bookings
  • Organize grad photo submissions for slipstick (yearbook) and composite
  • Responsible for CHBE slipstick (yearbook) contribution and submission
  • Work with EUS grad rep and Camp 5 for iron ring ceremony preparations
  • Organize one major grad event such as grad trip, grad dinner, boat cruise or any event recommended by graduating class
  • Participate in EUS grad committee meetings
