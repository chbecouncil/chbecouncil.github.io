Academimc Coordinator

Edward Wen (4th year) and Michael White (4th year)

Michael:

My name is Michael White and I am a third year chemical engineering student going into fourth year in September. I am currently involved with the Chem-E-Car design team and the UBC chess club. In my spare time I enjoy playing soccer and running. I want to be more involved in CHBE and the shared position of Academic Coordinator with Edward appeals to me because I have always found the Beef and Pizza sessions to be a unique and successful method of allowing students’ opinions to be heard.


Edward:

Hello! My name is Edward Wen and I am in third year CHBE, going into fourth year in the fall. Currently, I am involved with Chem-E-Car and I enjoy running, painting, and eating in my spare time. We all know how stressful school can be. Although students may talk amongst each other regarding shared concerns, the problem is usually not brought to light. I hope to change this by being the Academic Coordinator co-rep with Michael, and creating a more accommodating, and productive learning environment in CHBE.


Why are you a good fit for this position?

Edward and I are good friends whose skills complement each others nicely. This year we have worked together on the Chem-E-Car junior battery team, attending regular meeting and coming up with creative solutions to real world problems.
I believe our combined experience as two fourth years, one in chemical engineering and one in chemical + biological engineering will allow us to relate well with the students who are currently going through the same courses as us, or courses that we have previously taken.
I have prior experience emceeing assemblies in high school and would be confident leading the discussion at the Beef and Pizza sessions.
Edward and I know how challenging this program can be and will try to implement new ideas to ease the academic stress that comes with a full course load.

Election platform:
  • More input over pizza options. We will poll to determine what pizza establishment and toppings are desired for the meetings.
  • More regular beef and pizza sessions. We would ideally like to run one before first midterms and one before second midterms in each semester.
  • We will have a box at the Beef and Pizza sessions where people can drop in anonymous suggestions on pieces of paper as we know it can be a bit intimidating to raise your opinion in front of your classmates, friends and professors.
  • Edward and I will have a 24 hour response policy where CHBE students can email or Facebook message us their academic concerns and we will respond suggesting a solution or meeting time to further discuss their problem within 24 hours. We wish for students to bring their academic concerns throughout the term, not just at the Beef and Pizza sessions. We will be able to help students resolve their queries or direct them to the right people that can help them.
  • Edward and I will also periodically send out anonymous surveys regarding courses in the CHBE Facebook groups to gauge student feedback during the times between the Beef and Pizza sessions. If any issues are raised (e.g. too many deadlines in one week) we will work with the year reps and the professors to see if a solution can be reached.
  • Presidential Candidates have mentioned an interest in encouraging more group work within CHBE, and we believe this is a great idea. Ideally we could have a system with peer-to-peer tutoring for students where anyone can have their questions answered by their friends. Edward and I will be working with the new council to see what can be achieved.
