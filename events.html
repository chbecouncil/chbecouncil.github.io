  • Campaign period- March 1-10, 2017
  • Election speeches- March 6/8, 2017
  • Elections - March 9/10, 2017


2. Am I eligible to be nominated? Anyone who is enrolled as an undergraduate in CHBE is eligible

3. Can I run with a co-representative? Yes, a maximum of one other co-representative is allowed. You need only to submit ONE nomination for the both of you, but assign one as the point of contact for this election period.

4. I will be away on co-op, can I still run? Council rules indicate that one person must be available to attend meetings on campus during your term. If you are not in Vancouver and cannot attend meetings, your role must be forfeited.

However, if you are running with a co-representative who will be in Vancouver to fulfill these duties, then YES you can still run. As long as you coordinate this in advance (i.e., one co-rep will be in Vancouver for September 2017, and the other will be around for January 2018).

5. How long are election speeches? Maximum of 3 minutes for speeches, and 2 minutes for Q&A. This is still subject to change.

6. What is the time commitment per role? Time commitment will vary depending on how much you want to contribute and what your role is! If you want to find out more, contact anyone on the council about their specific role. You can find their contact information on the “Team” page.

AIChE Annual Student Conference

Student engineers from more than 150 schools celebrate the Chemical Engineering profession, along with young professional members, AIChE leaders, and industry professionals from numerous engineering specialties.

Source: AIChE.org

This year, the UBC Chem-E-Car team competed against 40 other teams from arond the world and won first place for their poster.

Two students, Athanasios Kritharis and Siang Lim, were recognized for their outstanding academic records and received the AIChE Freshman and Donald F. Othmer Sophomore Academic Excellence Recognition Award respectivey!

Third-year student, Haider Kamal took part in the poster competition where he presented his work on a novel ocular drug delivery mechanism. Competing against over 150 other posters in his division, Haider took home second place. Congratulations!
